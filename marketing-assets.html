Draft the first channel assets

Use the approved campaign direction to create channel-native starting points. The goal is not final copy. The goal is a first set the team can review and refine.

Workflow

Scale one idea across paid, lifecycle, social, and web.

Codex can preserve the campaign spine while adapting tone and format to different channels. This shows why the brief and handoff matter.

Inputs

What to use

  • The campaign brief and chosen creative world
  • The production handoff and channel asks
  • Claims rules, product proof, and review snippets
  • The offer and landing-page role in the campaign
Outputs

What learners should get

  • Search and display ad copy
  • Meta feed, story, and Reels variants
  • TikTok creator hooks
  • Email starters, landing-page copy, and image prompts

Copy prompt

Handoff To Channel Assets

Using the brief and handoff, draft a first-pass channel kit: search ads, display copy, Meta feed/story/Reels variants, TikTok creator hooks, email subject lines and preview text, landing page hero copy, and image prompts. Keep the voice consistent, stay inside the guardrails, and flag anything that needs review.
Human review moment: Check whether every channel still sounds like the same campaign. If one channel drifts, ask Codex to revise it against the brief and guardrails.
