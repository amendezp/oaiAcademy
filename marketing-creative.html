Step 2 of 5

Turn the brief into a creative world

Now that the strategy is clear, use Codex to explore the campaign's creative range: mood, visual language, sample lines, and the direction worth taking forward.

Workflow

Move from strategic brief to creative territory.

A good creative direction gives the team taste and constraints. It should make the work feel easier to recognize, not just easier to write.

Inputs

What to use

  • The campaign brief from Workflow 1
  • Brand voice and visual reference assets
  • Claims guardrails and customer language
  • The audience insight and message ladder
Outputs

What learners should get

  • A few distinct creative worlds
  • Visual mood, texture, photography, and color direction
  • Sample campaign lines and image prompts
  • A recommended direction with rationale

Step by step

Run the workflow

  1. Step 1

    Review the brief

    Confirm the brief has a clear audience, insight, product story, and claims boundaries.

  2. Step 2

    Paste the prompt below

    Ask Codex for multiple creative worlds, then choose or adjust the one with the strongest fit.

  3. Step 3

    Steer taste

    If it gets generic, ask for a version that feels more specific to the brand and audience.

Copy prompt

Brief To Creative World

Take the brief and explore a few creative worlds for the campaign. Give each one a moodboard direction, a visual language, a few sample lines, and a clear reason it could work. Recommend your favorite.
Human review moment: Pick the direction that feels ownable. Codex can explore options quickly, but the team still decides what has taste, edge, and brand fit.
