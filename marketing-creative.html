Step 2 of 5

Turn the brief into a creative world

Now that the strategy is clear, use Codex to explore creative options: moodboard direction, visual language, sample lines, and the direction to review.

Inputs
  • The campaign brief from Workflow 1
  • Brand voice and visual reference assets
  • Claims guardrails and customer language
  • The audience insight and message ladder
Outputs
  • A few distinct creative worlds
  • Visual mood, texture, photography, and color direction
  • Sample campaign lines and image prompts
  • A recommended direction with rationale

Step by step

Run the workflow

0 of 3 completed
  1. Step 1

    Review the brief

    Confirm the brief has a clear audience, insight, product story, and claims boundaries.

  2. Step 2

    Paste the prompt below

    Ask Codex for multiple creative worlds, then choose or adjust the one with the strongest fit.

    Copy prompt

    Brief To Creative World

    Take the brief and explore a few creative worlds for the campaign. Give each one a moodboard direction, a visual language, a few sample lines, and a clear reason it could work. Recommend your favorite.
  3. Step 3

    Refine the direction

    After choosing a territory, ask Codex to make it more ownable to the brand and audience.

    Copy prompt

    Make It More Ownable

    Take the direction we picked and make it sharper. Give me 3 ways to make it feel more ownable for Chill Nest, then rewrite the moodboard direction and sample lines using the strongest one.

Workflow

Move from strategic brief to creative territory.

A good creative direction gives the team shared choices and constraints. It should make the work easier to recognize, not just easier to write.

Human review moment: Pick the direction that best fits the brief. Codex can explore options quickly, but the team still decides what has the right tone and brand fit.
