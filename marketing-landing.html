Step 5 of 5

Create the promotion landing page

Close the loop by asking Codex to turn the campaign kit into a responsive page the team can preview. This shows the handoff becoming an actual customer-facing artifact.

Workflow

Turn the campaign kit into a page people can inspect.

The landing page should carry the same strategy, voice, offer, proof, and visual direction from the earlier workflows.

Inputs

What to use

  • The campaign brief, creative world, and handoff
  • The channel kit and image prompts
  • Product facts, offer details, reviews, and FAQs
  • Brand and claims guardrails
Outputs

What learners should get

  • A responsive landing page preview
  • Hero, offer, proof, review, FAQ, and CTA sections
  • Visual styling aligned to the creative direction
  • Review notes for claims and final human approval

Step by step

Run the workflow

  1. Step 1

    Keep the campaign context

    Run this after the channel kit so Codex can reuse the brief, creative direction, and campaign assets.

  2. Step 2

    Paste the prompt below

    Ask Codex to create something previewable, not just a written recommendation.

  3. Step 3

    Inspect the page

    Review mobile fit, visual hierarchy, CTA clarity, claims language, and whether the page matches the campaign direction.

Copy prompt

Promotion Landing Page

Turn the campaign kit into a promotion landing page I can preview. Use the chosen creative direction, include the offer, product proof, reviews, FAQs, and clear CTAs, and make it responsive. Keep the page launch-ready, but mark anything that still needs human approval.

Before calling it done

Human review moment: Codex can build the first page fast. Marketing still owns the taste, claims, offer, conversion logic, and final launch decision.
